Adventure

Adventure genre consists of books where the protagonist goes on an epic journey, either personally or geographically. Often the protagonist has a mission and faces many obstacles in his way. A good example would be The Lord of the Rings series. The adventure genre often pairs up with the action genre, since long epic journeys have a lot of action in them. The adventure genre can be combined with almost any other genre; it's versatile like romance and drama in that it can be meshed with almost any other genre and still work.

Fantasy

A subgenre of speculative fiction that revolves around magic or supernatural forces, not technology. Stories from the fantasy genre can be set in fanciful, invented worlds or in a legendary, mythic past that rely on the outright invention of magic. Mystery

A genre that contains puzzles, the reader is given one piece of at a time to try and guess the big picture. A very versatile genre, The most commn sub genre on the market is the Murder Mistery. But it can be combined with fantasy, science fiction etc...

Romance

A genre that place the primary focus on the relationship and romantic love between two or more people. There is always conflict that hinders the relationship, but it gemnerally resolves to a "happy" ending. Can be meshed with almost any other genre.

Science Fiction

A sub genere of speculative fiction that involves strong elements of technology that are explained with thorough details and is plausible. Stories generally revolve around the ramifications of said "technology".

Western

Originally stories set in the American Old West. With bounty hunters, gunfighters, outlaws, setllers brothels, Indians, and gambling. Can be combined with other genres to bring the American Old west into new climates. The TV series Firefly is a good example of a 'Space Western'.
